Getting Started

Absolute Beginner Quick Start

Zero experience with Touch Portal? This gets you to a working test in three steps.

What this plugin actually does

MIDI Pro turns Touch Portal buttons, sliders, and dials into a MIDI controller. Press a button, and it sends a real MIDI message to your DAW, synth, or any MIDI-listening software. It can also listen to MIDI coming in, and — its signature feature — automatically show you what was just sent, without needing your gear to echo anything back.

No hardware? No problem Install a free virtual MIDI cable: loopMIDI on Windows, or the built-in IAC Driver on macOS. See Installation for details.

Your first action, in five steps

Open a Touch Portal page and click a button to edit it.
In the Actions list, open the MIDI Pro category.
Drag MIDI Note onto the button.
Set MIDI Out to your virtual port or real device.
Press the button. If your DAW is listening on that port, a note comes in.

Core Concepts

Four building blocks worth understanding before anything else:

ConceptWhat it isExample
ActionA button you press. Sends one MIDI message type.MIDI Note on a Play button
ConnectorA slider or dial you drag. Sends continuous values.CC Fader bound to a volume slider
StateA live value Touch Portal remembers.tx.cc.volume.value
EventA trigger that fires the instant something happens.MIDI CC Sent
This only happens if you name it States and events only exist for controls you've given a Name. Leave it blank and the control still sends MIDI perfectly — it just publishes nothing.
